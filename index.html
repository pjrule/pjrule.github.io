Parker J. Rule

I am an undergraduate at Tufts University, where I study mathematics, computer science, and STS (science, technology, and society). I work as a software engineer and research associate at MGGG Redistricting Lab, a research group within Tufts’ Tisch College of Civic Life led by Moon Duchin. At the lab, I work on a range of problems in scientific computing and operations research. My recent projects include

  • A model used to schedule COVID-19 tests for the Tufts population.
  • A model for allocating unused dormitory beds to strained hospitals.
  • GerryChain.jl, a high-performance Julia library for Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling of redistricting plans.
  • An interactive tool based on queueing theory used by university administrators to design COVID-19 testing centers.

I am also affiliated with Tufts’ Computational Geometry research group.

selected publications

  1. Geometry of graph partitions via optimal transport
    Tara Abrishami, Nestor Guillen, Parker Rule, Zachary Schutzman, Justin Solomon, Thomas Weighill, and Si Wu
    SIAM Journal on Scientific Computing, 2020.
    Abs arXiv PDF Code

    We define a distance metric between partitions of a graph using machinery from optimal transport. Our metric is built from a linear assignment problem that matches partition components, with assignment cost proportional to transport distance over graph edges. We show that our distance can be computed using a single linear program without precomputing pairwise assignment costs and derive several theoretical properties of the metric. Finally, we provide experiments demonstrating these properties empirically, specifically focusing on the metric’s value for new problems in ensemble-based analysis of political districting plans.
